BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS BY CUSTOMER SEGMENT
(Based on actual analysis of 5,630 e-commerce customers)

────────────────────────────────────────────────

NEEDS ATTENTION (27% churn rate, lowest CLV) ⚠️ TOP PRIORITY
  → Immediate intervention: targeted promotions, satisfaction surveys
  → These customers are the LEAST predictable — middle-ground behavior
    that could go either way
  → Highest churn rate in our data — most urgent group to act on
  → Walmart parallel: occasional shoppers who haven't decided if they
    prefer us over competitors yet

NEW CUSTOMERS (22% churn rate, low CLV)
  → Onboarding campaign: welcome emails, first-purchase discounts,
    in-app tutorials
  → Critical window — make their first experience great or they leave
    before reaching profitability
  → BIGGEST OPPORTUNITY: this is the largest segment in our data, so
    even a small improvement in retention has massive impact
  → Walmart parallel: first-time grocery pickup users — if their first
    order goes wrong, they don't come back

AT RISK (18% churn rate, moderate CLV)
  → Personalized retention offers: free shipping, exclusive deals
  → These customers used to buy frequently but have gone quiet — they
    still remember us, so re-engagement works
  → Walmart parallel: a regular weekly shopper who suddenly stopped
    coming after a bad experience

CHAMPIONS (15% churn rate, highest CLV ~$1300)
  → Reward and retain: loyalty programs, early access, VIP treatment
  → Small group but extremely valuable — losing one Champion hurts
    more than losing 10 New Customers
  → Walmart parallel: Walmart+ subscribers who shop weekly and use
    multiple services

LOYAL (12% churn rate, very high CLV ~$1050)
  → Maintain engagement: occasional rewards, referral programs
  → LARGEST high-value segment in our data — the foundation of revenue
  → Don't over-invest, but never ignore
  → Walmart parallel: long-time grocery customers who buy the same
    things every week

LOST (10% churn rate, lowest CLV)
  → Minimal investment: a "we miss you" email at most
  → Note: counterintuitively, these have the LOWEST current churn rate
    in our data — likely because the truly lost customers already left.
    The remaining ones are stable infrequent buyers.
  → Retention cost would exceed their value
  → Walmart parallel: someone who visited once a month for a specific
    item and that's all they need

────────────────────────────────────────────────

KEY INSIGHTS:

1. "Loyal" customers are the GOLD — high value, low churn, and they
   form the largest valuable segment. Protect them at all costs.

2. The biggest opportunity is "New Customers" — large group with high
   churn AND low CLV. This means most newcomers leave before becoming
   valuable. Fix onboarding and unlock massive value by moving them
   into the "Loyal" segment.

3. "Needs Attention" should get the most urgent action because they
   churn at the highest rate (27%), even though their individual value
   is lower than Champions or Loyal.

4. Counterintuitively, "Lost" customers in this dataset have the lowest
   current churn rate. Real-world insight: by the time a customer is
   truly "lost," they've already left the system entirely. The dataset
   only contains customers still active enough to be measured.

5. Not all churn is worth preventing. A retention offer must cost less
   than the customer's lifetime value. Focus retention budgets where
   the math works: At Risk and Needs Attention segments.
